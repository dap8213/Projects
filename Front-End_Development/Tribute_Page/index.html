Dr. Martin Luther King Jr.

The Man With A Dream

Martin Luther King leading march from Selma to Montgomery to protest lack of voting rights.

Here's a time line of Dr. Martin Luther King Jr:

  • 1929 - Born in Atlanta, Georgia
  • 1932 - January: King begins nursery school
  • 1935 - Martin attends Elementary School
  • 1942 - Martin attends High School
  • 1944 - Martin Luther King attends College
  • 1946 - The National Committee on Civil Rights is created
  • 1947 - Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. decided to become a minister
  • 1948 - King graduates from Morehouse College in Atlanta with a B.A. in Sociology
  • 1951 - He graduates from Crozer with a Bachelor of Divinity degree
  • 1953 - The first bus boycott starts in Baton Rouge, Louisiana
  • 1955 - King earns his Ph.D. in Systematic Theology from Boston University
  • 1957 - The Southern Christian Leadership Conference (SCLC) is formed and Dr. King is elected as president
  • 1958 - Dr. King meets with President Eisenhower
  • 1961 -Martin Luther King meets with President Kennedy to gain his support for the civil rights movement
  • 1963 - President John F. Kennedy is assassinated
  • 1964 - Martin Luther King becomes the youngest person to receive the Nobel Peace Prize
  • 1965 - King and 25,000 other protestors march from Selma to Montgomery for voting rights
  • 1968 - While standing on the balcony of his room at the Lorraine Motel in Memphis, Tennessee Dr. Martin Luther King is shot and killed
  • 1977 - He was posthumously awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by President Jimmy Carter
  • 1986 - Martin Luther King, Jr. Day was established as a national holiday in the United States
